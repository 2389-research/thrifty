# The Bones of Saltrest

*A D&D 5e adventure hook for a party of level 5. (atelier split-tier build.)*

## The Hook

Salt crusts the railings of the Salted Anchor's dock-facing windows. The tavern's common room sits half-full, patrons nursing their ale with the tight shoulders of people waiting for bad news. You smell brine and wet wood, the ordinary reek of a fishing village—except the boats are in, and the nets hang idle.

Mayor Halrik has been gone for seven days. He rowed north alone toward the caves, told no one why, and the village has wound tight as a rope since his departure. At night, the breakwater groans with a sound that isn't wind or tide. The water offshore has turned the wrong color—not grey, not green, but a sickly slate that doesn't catch the light right. Mother Vell, the village priest, will not go near it. She speaks of wrongness in the water, of whispers that aren't quite voices, and she keeps the temple doors locked at dusk.

In the Salted Anchor's corner, one of the dock hands mentions he saw someone buying rope and lanterns from the merchant three days before Halrik vanished—a woman with the look of someone who knows how to work in the dark. No one speaks her name.

Mother Vell herself finds you where you're lodging. Her hands shake as she begs you to find the Mayor, or at least to see what he found in the north-shore caves. The village cannot survive much longer like this. The water is calling him home, she whispers, but home is not a place anymore.

## Cast & Factions

### Mayor Halrik (Antagonist)

Halrik vanished a week ago, rowing alone into the black water toward the north-shore caves. His motivation is desperation: he seeks a cure for his daughter's wasting sickness, and rumor spoke of the drowned healer's knowledge dwelling in those depths. You find his abandoned rowboat half-submerged in the cove. When encountered, he is broken—eyes milky, lips salt-crusted—speaking in a voice like wind through a wreck, no longer quite himself.

### Mother Vell (Ally)

The village priest refuses to touch the water anymore, not since the drowning began. Her motivation is clear and fierce: protect Saltrest from the drowned and uncover what called them up. She can provide the party with records of the village's dead from the past three seasons and the names of those lost at sea. Her hands shake when she speaks of the water, but her faith anchors the living.

### Coria Sleet (Ally)

Coria is a necromancer driven by mercy, not malice. She binds the drowned not to harm but to heal: these souls are trapped in agony, tethered to this world, and she intends to lay them to rest. Her method is to recover the **Seraphine ledger**—a merchant's manifest of 143 names, the crew lost in the wreck that founded their drowning—and read each name aloud as a final remembrance. Last seen buying rope and lanterns in the village, she is a woman of ash-pale skin and tired eyes, and witnesses report she speaks to the water in a language like singing.

### The Drowned Choir (Antagonist)

A rising chorus of sailors, bloated and pale, their limbs dragging through the shallows. They sing discordantly—a sound like the groaning of hull timbers and the rush of drowning breath. Though they hunger for the living, they are slaves to Coria's ritual, compelled to protect her work in the caves until the ledger is read and they are freed. They embody both the village's immediate threat and its path to peace.

## Kelpwater Cove

Three miles north of Saltrest, where sea-cliffs wall the shore, Kelpwater Cove is a tidal warren of caves reeking of brine and rot. Mayor Halrik came here a week ago and met Coria Sleet, a necromancer bent on mercy: reading the drowned names from the Seraphine ledger, granting them rest. The Drowned Choir now guards these depths.

### Level 1 — The Silt Chambers

Brackish water floods the upper cave ankle-deep, thick with salt mist and writhing kelp. Six drowned sailors patrol here, each with AC 13, 9 HP. They respond to Coria's chant and attack anything above the water-line.

The floor is treacherous; a character moving at half speed or faster must succeed on a DC 12 Dexterity (Acrobatics) check or fall prone. Iron spikes and shale shards lie beneath the water.

A DC 13 Wisdom (Perception) check reveals a side passage hidden by kelp, sloping dry and downward.

### Level 2 — Drowned Treasury

A vast submerged chamber, once a merchant's stronghold, now drowned for centuries. The water is 8 feet deep and viciously cold. Any creature beginning its turn submerged must succeed on a DC 13 Constitution (Endurance) check or gain one level of exhaustion.

A revenant captain (AC 15, 35 HP) and a drowned giant (AC 12, 28 HP) hold this level. The captain guards the descent; the giant anchors a choke-point passage strewn with ship's rigging requiring a DC 14 Strength (Athletics) check to pass without entanglement.

A DC 14 Intelligence (Investigation) check reveals an intact ship's anchor in the wreckage—heavy enough to weight the Seraphine ledger in the climax.

### Level 3 — The Vault

A flooded cathedral with luminescent water cast by Coria's necromantic essence. At the center pedestal lies the Seraphine ledger: a manifest of 143 drowned names, preserved by dark magic. Coria stands beside it, neither attacking nor fleeing. The Drowned Choir—two dozen thralls—stand motionless in the glow. They will not attack unless the party moves toward the ledger.

The party may reach the ledger by two routes:

**The Direct Path:** Force through the water and Choir. The water near the pedestal is 10 feet deep. A character must succeed on a DC 15 Strength (Athletics) check to swim it in one round without exhaustion. A leviathan horror (AC 16, 42 HP) chained to the pedestal attacks if the party reaches within 15 feet of the ledger.

**The Tidal Route:** A DC 14 Wisdom (Survival) check reveals a narrow siphon in the eastern wall that empties directly into the Vault behind the leviathan's chain. This requires a DC 14 Dexterity (Acrobatics) check to navigate silently. It is passable only at low tide; at high tide (every 6 hours), it becomes impassable.

Coria will not fight. If spoken with, she explains her purpose: grant the drowned peace by reading the manifest, releasing them from thralldom. She views theft as violation; co-reading as fellowship.

### The Ticking Clock — Seraphine Ledger

When the party submerges the Seraphine ledger in saltwater with intent to destroy it, the clock begins.

**Round 5:** The ledger becomes fully submerged. Any character wishing to recover it must succeed on a DC 15 Strength (Athletics) check. Success pulls it from the water, brine-sodden but intact. Failure means it sinks further.

**Round 10:** The ledger dissolves completely. The binding crumbles, the pages scatter into pulp, and the names wash into the sea. The "read the names aloud" path to mercy is permanently closed.

Announce the round count aloud each turn to keep the urgency felt at the table.

## Resolution

### Path 1 — Combat: Silence the Choir

The party descends into Kelpwater Cove and engages the Drowned Choir in the Vault. When the final thrall sinks and the chamber falls silent, the corpses drift back into the depths, chains dissolving into brine. Coria, seeing her charges slaughtered, weeps openly and surrenders without resistance. Mayor Halrik sinks with the Choir. For their valor, the party discovers a corroded silver coffer among the wrecked timbers: **800 gold pieces**. The village is spared, though Halrik's fate weighs heavy.

### Path 2 — Town Defense: Hold the Breakwater

The party fortifies Saltrest and holds the breakwater against the Choir's assault. When the leviathan horror dies in the Vault, it severs the binding to Coria's ritual; the remaining thralls sink back into the depths. Mother Vell leads the rites of cleansing. The cost is stark: twelve villagers fall, their names carved into the temple's memorial. In gratitude, Saltrest grants the party salvage rights to a merchant's cache that washed ashore: **600 gold pieces and three masterwork fishing hooks**. Saltrest survives, but diminished.

### Path 3 — Mercy: Read the Names Aloud

The party chooses to help Coria grant the drowned peace by recovering and reading the Seraphine ledger aloud.

**Recovery:** The clock begins when the ledger touches saltwater with intent to destroy it. By **round 5** it is fully submerged—recovering it then requires a **DC 15 Strength (Athletics) check** (success pulls it free, brine-sodden but intact; failure means it sinks further). If it is not recovered by **round 10**, the ledger dissolves completely—pages scatter into pulp, names wash into the sea. This path closes forever.

**Reading:** If recovered in time, a character reads all 143 names aloud while the Choir presses in. With each name spoken, a thrall's eyes clear and they sink gently back into rest. The water grows calmer. By the final name, the Vault is silent. The leviathan horror stills and sinks. Coria stands witness, tears streaming, her work completed. She does not resist but offers the party a boon: knowledge of the Seraphine wreck's location, deep below the cove. With that intelligence, the party can ensure no future necromancer disturbs those depths with greed or malice.

### Aftermath

Whichever path you choose, Saltrest endures. The sea does not forget, but the village's boats return to the nets, the Salted Anchor reopens its doors, and Mother Vell tends her temple with prayers both hopeful and wary. The drowned have had their due.
