## The Hook

Salt crusts the railings of the Salted Anchor's dock-facing windows. The tavern's common room sits half-full, patrons nursing their ale with the tight shoulders of people waiting for bad news. You smell brine and wet wood, the ordinary reek of a fishing village—except the boats are in, and the nets hang idle.

Mayor Halrik has been gone for seven days. He rowed north alone toward the caves, told no one why, and the village has wound tight as a rope since his departure. At night, the breakwater groans with a sound that isn't wind or tide. The water offshore has turned the wrong color—not grey, not green, but a sickly slate that doesn't catch the light right. Mother Vell, the village priest, will not go near it. She speaks of wrongness in the water, of whispers that aren't quite voices, and she keeps the temple doors locked at dusk.

In the Salted Anchor's corner, one of the dock hands mentions he saw someone buying rope and lanterns from the merchant three days before Halrik vanished—a woman with the look of someone who knows how to work in the dark. No one speaks her name.

Mother Vell herself finds you where you're lodging. Her hands shake as she begs you to find the Mayor, or at least to see what he found in the north-shore caves. The village cannot survive much longer like this. The water is calling him home, she whispers, but home is not a place anymore.
