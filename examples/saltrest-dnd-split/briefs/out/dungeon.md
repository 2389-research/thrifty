## Kelpwater Cove

Three miles north of Saltrest, where sea-cliffs wall the shore, Kelpwater Cove is a tidal warren of caves reeking of brine and rot. Mayor Halrik came here a week ago and met Coria Sleet, a necromancer bent on mercy: reading the drowned names from the Seraphine ledger, granting them rest. The Drowned Choir now guards these depths.

### Level 1 — The Silt Chambers

Brackish water floods the upper cave ankle-deep, thick with salt mist and writhing kelp. Six drowned sailors patrol here, each with AC 13, 9 HP. They respond to Coria's chant and attack anything above the water-line.

The floor is treacherous; a character moving at half speed or faster must succeed on a DC 12 Dexterity (Acrobatics) check or fall prone. Iron spikes and shale shards lie beneath the water.

A DC 13 Wisdom (Perception) check reveals a side passage hidden by kelp, sloping dry and downward.

### Level 2 — Drowned Treasury

A vast submerged chamber, once a merchant's stronghold, now drowned for centuries. The water is 8 feet deep and viciously cold. Any creature beginning its turn submerged must succeed on a DC 13 Constitution (Endurance) check or gain one level of exhaustion.

A revenant captain (AC 15, 35 HP) and a drowned giant (AC 12, 28 HP) hold this level. The captain guards the descent; the giant anchors a choke-point passage strewn with ship's rigging requiring a DC 14 Strength (Athletics) check to pass without entanglement.

A DC 14 Intelligence (Investigation) check reveals an intact ship's anchor in the wreckage—heavy enough to weight the Seraphine ledger in the climax.

### Level 3 — The Vault

A flooded cathedral with luminescent water cast by Coria's necromantic essence. At the center pedestal lies the Seraphine ledger: a manifest of 143 drowned names, preserved by dark magic. Coria stands beside it, neither attacking nor fleeing. The Drowned Choir—two dozen thralls—stand motionless in the glow. They will not attack unless the party moves toward the ledger.

The party may reach the ledger by two routes:

**The Direct Path:** Force through the water and Choir. The water near the pedestal is 10 feet deep. A character must succeed on a DC 15 Strength (Athletics) check to swim it in one round without exhaustion. A leviathan horror (AC 16, 42 HP) chained to the pedestal attacks if the party reaches within 15 feet of the ledger.

**The Tidal Route:** A DC 14 Wisdom (Survival) check reveals a narrow siphon in the eastern wall that empties directly into the Vault behind the leviathan's chain. This requires a DC 14 Dexterity (Acrobatics) check to navigate silently. It is passable only at low tide; at high tide (every 6 hours), it becomes impassable.

Coria will not fight. If spoken with, she explains her purpose: grant the drowned peace by reading the manifest, releasing them from thralldom. She views theft as violation; co-reading as fellowship.

### The Ticking Clock — Seraphine Ledger

When the party submerges the Seraphine ledger in saltwater with intent to destroy it, the clock begins.

**Round 5:** The ledger becomes fully submerged. Any character wishing to recover it must succeed on a DC 15 Strength (Athletics) check. Success pulls it from the water, brine-sodden but intact. Failure means it sinks further.

**Round 10:** The ledger dissolves completely. The binding crumbles, the pages scatter into pulp, and the names wash into the sea. The "read the names aloud" path to mercy is permanently closed.

Announce the round count aloud each turn to keep the urgency felt at the table.
